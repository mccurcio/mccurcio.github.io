As a student, I studied biochemistry, worked with live animals, mammalian cell culture, carried out biochemical and immunological tests, all in a chemistry department. My professor's lab was eclectic. We were all proud that we had our 'fingers in many pies.'

After graduating, I was thrilled to work in biotechnology laboratories applying what I had learned. My work included vaccine production, growing transfected cell lines producing biologically active molecules, and purifying those targets for therapeutic testing.  Over time, I gained experience with many different pieces of scientific equipment. Due to that knowledge of equipment, my work shifted to technical applications and sales of scientific equipment. 

Training researchers of all levels, I found this opened my eye to teaching. Connecting with students in an informal setting was a craft I learned and grew to enjoy. Becoming a certified teacher allowed me to work with kindergarten to high school students. Working with children from inner-city neighborhoods is trying but rewarding. Seeing children at the beginning of the day was exhilarating. I hoped I could connect with my students or at least that they would make me laugh.

After some time, I realized I loved science too much to leave it. My knowledge of computers introduced me to the field of Data Science and web development. My goal for the past year has been to teach myself these areas. With my strong background in Statistics, I was better able to understand machine learning. With my understanding of Python, I am working on Django and its many facets. My current project is to create a booklet comparing different machine learning techniques using R, primarily with minor use of Python.
